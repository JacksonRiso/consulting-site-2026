  What I would do is separate the actual delivery model from the way you message it.

  How I’d position it
  On the landing page, sell this as:

  - A free 30-minute diagnostic call
  - Fast initial value
  - A hands-on embedded operator/consultant model
  - Optional 3-month engagement as the recommended starting structure

  That means your landing page should say something like:

  Book a free 30-minute operations diagnostic.
  Bring me the bottlenecks, broken workflows, and team frustrations. I’ll help you identify what’s actually wrong and where the fastest wins are.
  If there’s a fit, I’ll recommend a hands-on engagement where I onboard quickly, make changes directly, and train your team so the improvements stick.

  That is cleaner and lower-friction than leading with “three months.”

  How I’d describe the 3-month model
  Do not frame it as a rigid minimum first. Frame it as the best structure for durable results.

  Example:

  - Most clients start with a 3-month engagement.
  - Month 1: Learn the business, systems, and failure points
  - Month 2: Implement and stabilize the highest-impact changes
  - Month 3: Validate results, refine the workflow, and train the team
  - After that, continue month-to-month as needed

  That does two things:

  - preserves your qualification filter
  - makes the offer feel methodical instead of heavy

  What to emphasize more
  Your best landing-page angle is not “I need 3 months.”
  It is:

  - I usually find leverage quickly
  - I can often implement meaningful change in the first 2 weeks
  - I don’t just advise; I implement and train
  - Your team actually learns the new system

  That last point matters a lot. “I love training your team” is a strong differentiator because many consultants stop at recommendations.

  I would explicitly add:
  I handle the work from diagnosis to implementation to team training, so you’re not left with a slide deck and no follow-through.
