The Problem

Growth tends to expose the cracks in how work gets done.

What used to work starts feeling fragile.

Reporting gets messy. Handoffs break. The team relies on workarounds. Important tasks start slipping through the cracks. Small changes feel riskier than they should. Smart people spend too much time compensating for systems that are no longer doing their job.

At that point, the issue is usually not effort. It is not that the team suddenly became less capable. It is usually a process and systems problem.

That is the kind of problem I help solve.

Common symptoms

  • Reporting is inconsistent or hard to trust
  • Work gets dropped between teams or tools
  • Manual work keeps piling up
  • The process depends on tribal knowledge
  • Small changes feel weirdly dangerous
  • The business is growing, but the system behind it is not

How I Work

I start by figuring out what is actually broken.

I usually work with one person who sees the bigger picture and can help set priorities, and one person who is closer to the day-to-day work and feels the pain of the current process directly.

From there, I identify the real bottleneck, simplify the process behind it, implement the changes directly, document the system clearly, and train the team so the improvements hold up.

I also meet weekly with clients to review progress, talk through what has been accomplished, and align on what comes next.

I do not just hand over recommendations. I help make the system better and help the team use it well.
