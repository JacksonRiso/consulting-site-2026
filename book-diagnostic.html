FAQ

Questions before you book?

Is this really free?

Yes. The initial 30-minute diagnostic is free.

Do I need to prepare anything?

No formal prep is required. Just be ready to explain what feels broken, where the team is getting stuck, and what is creating the most frustration right now.

What happens on the first call?

You walk me through what feels broken, fragile, or harder than it should be. My job is to help you get clearer on what is actually going wrong and whether there is an obvious next move.

Will you pitch me on the call?

If it seems like I can help, I will explain what working together could look like. But the first goal of the call is to understand the problem and add value, not force a fit.

What happens after the first 30-minute diagnostic?

About 80% of the time, potential clients do not actually need my help. They just need to make one simple change. If that is the case, I will tell you what to do and send you off to go make it. If it looks like I can help more directly, I will explain what a good next step would be.

How quickly can we start if there is a fit?

Currently, I onboard one client per month. If there is a fit, I try to get into the business quickly so I can start learning the system, meeting the right people, and identifying the first areas to improve.

How involved do I or my team need to be?

You do not need to be involved in every detail, but I do need enough access to understand the business, align on priorities, and get feedback as the work progresses. The goal is not to create more work for you. The goal is to make the right changes with the right level of visibility.

Ideally, I work with one person who sees the bigger picture, understands the company goals, and can help me set priorities, and one person who is closer to the day-to-day work and can give direct feedback on what is not working.

Do you only advise, or do you actually implement the changes?

I implement the changes directly. I do not just identify what should happen. I help make it happen, document it clearly, and train the team so the improvements hold up.
